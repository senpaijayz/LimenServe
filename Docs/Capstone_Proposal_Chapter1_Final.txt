2.1 INTRODUCTION

The rapid advancement of information technology has transformed how businesses manage daily operations across industries. This capstone project focuses on the development of a web-based management information system (MIS) designed to modernize retail and service workflows for businesses providing genuine Mitsubishi car parts. By integrating automated cost estimation, quotation generation, barcode technology, a public customer portal, and three-dimensional spatial visualization, the system seeks to automate inventory tracking, sales processing, and service order management while providing an intuitive digital representation of the physical stockroom.

The study addresses the common operational challenges faced by small enterprises, particularly the inefficiencies found in manual, paper-based record-keeping, the difficulty of locating items in complex storage layouts, and the time-consuming process of generating price quotations for customers. These traditional methods often lead to delays in service, inaccuracies in stock monitoring, and inconsistent pricing estimates, which impact the overall productivity of the business.

The aim of this study is to provide a comprehensive digital solution that streamlines transaction handling, optimizes inventory organization, and professionalizes the quotation and service order process. By applying modern software development practices and spatial mapping techniques, the project seeks to enhance record accuracy, reduce retrieval time, and support data-driven decision-making for both product sales and service operations.

2.1.1 Project Context

Limen Auto Parts Center is an established family-owned auto parts retail shop located at 1308, 264 Epifanio de los Santos Avenue, Pasay City, Metro Manila. Since its inception thirteen years ago, the business has focused on providing genuine Mitsubishi car parts that are widely available and sought after in the Philippines. The shop is managed by Mr. Wilson Limen and Mrs. Angelica Limen, supported by their children and a dedicated staff of four individuals, for a total workforce of six members who handle sales, customer service, and stock management.

The physical layout of the shop consists of a two-floor commercial space. The first floor is utilized as the main sales area, while the second floor serves as the stockroom. Currently, all operational records—including inventory logs, sales receipts, and service orders—are paper-based. This project ensures that every single paper record is transformed into a digital database entry.

This reliance on manual operations has created several critical issues in the shop's daily activities. One common scenario occurs when a customer inquires about the estimated cost for a repair job including parts; staff members must manually calculate the total, often leading to slow response times and inconsistent quotes. Furthermore, when verifying stock availability, staff are often required to physically navigate between the two floors, leading to significant customer waiting times. The lack of real-time visibility often results in "blind stockouts," where an item is listed as available in the manual log but is discovered to be missing only during the physical retrieval process.

The absence of a structured spatial mapping system also complicates the training of new staff and slows down item retrieval for experienced employees. Currently, the organization of parts across the two floors relies heavily on the memory of the long-term staff. A particularly repetitive struggle involves the issuance of sales receipts and service quotations; the business must constantly purchase physical booklets, and staff members are burdened with the manual transcription of every item and service. When sales volumes are high, the time spent manually calculating daily totals and transcribing these handwritten receipts into weekly reports becomes a major bottleneck, often leading to human errors and delayed identification of inventory movement patterns. These scenarios highlight the urgent need for a digitized system that integrates physical location data, automated cost estimation, and real-time transaction processing.

2.1.2 Objectives of the Study

The general objective of this study is to design, develop, test, and evaluate LimenServe: A Web-Based Management Information System with Automated Cost Estimation and Quotation for Services and Orders of Limen Auto Parts Center.

The specific objectives are as follows:

1. Design the system by specifying its core capabilities and functional modules, which include:
   - Automated Cost Estimation and Quotation: To generate instant price estimates for customer inquiries involving parts and services.
   - Public Customer Portal: To empower customers to browse inventory and generate their own cost estimates online, serving as a quotation tool only.
   - Centralized Database: To fully digitize all sales records, receipts, and service history, eliminating paper dependencies.
   - Service Order Management: To track the status of service requests from intake to completion.
   - Barcode Scanning and Product Identification for rapid transaction entry.
   - Point of Sale (POS) Transaction Processing with automatic thermal receipt generation to eliminate manual writing.
   - Real-Time Inventory Tracking with automatic stock level adjustments.
   - 3D Visualization of Shop Layout to assist in physical item location.
   - Automated Report Generation for daily, weekly, and monthly business analysis.
   - Role-Based User Access Control for Admin, Cashier, and Stock Personnel.

2. Develop the system using appropriate hardware and software technologies:
   - Software Requirements: JavaScript (ES6+), HTML5, and CSS3. Key frameworks include React.js with Vite for the frontend, Node.js with Express.js for the backend API, Supabase (PostgreSQL) for the relational database, and Three.js for 3D spatial visualization.
   - Deployment: Vercel for frontend hosting, Render for backend API hosting, and Supabase for PostgreSQL database hosting.
   - Hardware Requirements: A computer system (minimum i5 processor, 8GB RAM), a barcode scanner (USB/Bluetooth), and a thermal receipt printer.

3. Test the system using comprehensive methodologies and tools to ensure functionality and reliability:
   - Unit Testing: Using the Jest framework to verify individual components.
   - Integration Testing: Using Supertest for API validation and Cypress for end-to-end testing.
   - User Acceptance Testing (UAT): Conducting hands-on sessions with Limen Auto Parts Center staff to validate real-world usability.

4. Evaluate the system based on the ISO 25010 Software Quality Model to determine its technical quality and user acceptability, focusing on characteristics such as:
   - Functional Suitability
   - Performance Efficiency
   - Usability
   - Reliability
   - Security
   - Maintainability

5. Prepare an implementation plan that outlines the transition from manual to digital operations, including deployment strategies and a user training program.

2.1.3 Purpose and Description

Purpose
The purpose of this study is to provide Limen Auto Parts Center with a practical and innovative tool to transition from paper-based management to a modernized digital ecosystem. Research is needed because the current manual workflow limits the business's ability to respond quickly to customer needs and maintain perfect stock accuracy. By implementing this system, the shop will derive practical benefits such as reduced operational errors, faster transaction times, and better spatial organization of their two-floor inventory.

Description of the Proposed System
The system is a web-integrated MIS designed to unify sales, inventory, and service management. Key capabilities include:
- Automated Cost Estimation and Quotation: Allows staff to quickly generate professional price quotes for customers, aggregating parts and service fees.
- Integrated POS and Barcode Support: Allows for seamless checkout and instant stock updates.
- 3D Spatial Visualization: Provides an interactive map of the stockroom, allowing users to "see" where items are located before physically going to get them.
- Automated Reporting: Eliminates manual math by automatically generating sales, service, and movement charts.

Beneficiaries
The study will be beneficial to the following:
- Limen Auto Parts Center Owners: Provides comprehensive business insight, from inventory valuation to service revenue tracking.
- Shop Employees: Reduces physical fatigue and mental workload by streamlining item locating, automating quote generation, and eliminating manual receipt writing.
- Customers: Ensures faster service, transparent pricing through printed quotations, and accurate parts availability.
- Student Proponents: Offers applied experience in developing complex business logic.

2.1.5 Scope and Limitation of the Study

Scope
The study covers the design, development, and implementation of a management information system specifically for the Pasay City branch of Limen Auto Parts Center. The specific scope includes:

1. User Management: Role-based access for Administrators, Stock Clerks, and Cashiers.
2. Public Customer Portal: A web interface allowing customers to view stock availability and generate service quotations (No payment collection).
3. Inventory Module: Real-time tracking of stock levels, low-stock alerts, and batch entry via barcode scanning.
4. Sales & Payment: Integrated POS interface for upfront physical store payments only; ensures every sale is saved to the central database.
4. Automated Cost Estimation & Quotation: Module for creating, printing, and saving service and parts estimates for customers.
5. Service Management: Tracking of service orders and basic job status monitoring.
6. Spatial Visualization: A web-based 3D digital twin of the 2-floor stockroom for visual item location.
7. Offline Capability: The system is designed with an Offline-First architecture, allowing core POS and Inventory references to function locally during internet outages.

Limitation
The study limits its coverage to the following constraints:

1. Cloud Sync Dependency: While core POS functions work offline, the remote owner dashboard require active internet connectivity to function and synchronize.
2. Hardware & Platform: The application is a Progressive Web App (PWA) optimized for desktop and tablet Chrome browsers and is not a native iOS/Android application. It assumes the use of standard reliable barcode scanners (HID mode).
3. Scope of Automation: The system automates digital record-keeping and calculation but still requires manual physical placement of items to match the 3D coordinates.
4. Estimation Accuracy: The Automated Cost Estimation provides values based on current pricelists and standard service fees; it may not account for unforeseen mechanical complications that arise during actual repair work.
5. System Boundary: It is designed for a single-branch operation; the Public Customer Portal is strictly for viewing and quotation generation.
6. Local Transaction Processing: The system enforces a strict "Upfront Payment" policy at the physical store; no online payments are processed.
7. Logistic Constraints: The system allows tagging orders for Metro Manila delivery but does not provide real-time GPS tracking or automated rider dispatching.

The final output is LimenServe: A Web-Based Management Information System with Automated Cost Estimation and Quotation for Services and Orders of Limen Auto Parts Center.

2.1.7 Definition of Terms

3D-Spatial Visualization: A digital, three-dimensional representation of the physical shop layout used to identify and manage the location of items.
Automated Cost Estimation: The system's ability to calculate total expected costs for parts and labor instantly based on selected items and service types.
Barcode Integration: The use of hardware scanners to read product codes for lightning-fast inventory and POS data entry.
Functional Suitability: An ISO 25010 metric that evaluates how well the system's functions meet the stated and implied needs of the user.
Point of Sale (POS): The specific location or interface where a retail transaction is completed and recorded.
Public Customer Portal: A web-accessible interface that allows external customers to view inventory and create cost estimates without logging in.
Quotation: A formal statement setting out the estimated cost for a particular job or service.
Spatial Management: The organization and optimization of products within the physical dimensions of the store, assisted by digital mapping.
Direct Stock Update: The automatic adjustment of inventory quantities as soon as a sale is finalized in the POS module.
