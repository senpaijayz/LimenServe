INTRODUCTION

The rapid advancement of information technology has transformed how businesses manage daily operations across industries. This capstone project focuses on the development of a web-based management information system (MIS) designed to modernize retail and service workflows for businesses providing genuine Mitsubishi car parts. By integrating automated cost estimation, quotation generation, barcode technology, a public customer portal, and three-dimensional spatial visualization, the system seeks to automate inventory tracking, sales processing, and service order management while providing an intuitive digital representation of the physical stockroom.
The study addresses the common operational challenges faced by small enterprises, particularly the inefficiencies found in manual, paper-based record-keeping, the difficulty of locating items in complex storage layouts, and the time-consuming process of generating price quotations for customers. These traditional methods often lead to delays in service, inaccuracies in stock monitoring, and inconsistent pricing estimates, which impact the overall productivity of the business.
The aim of this study is to provide a comprehensive digital solution that streamlines transaction handling, optimizes inventory organization, and professionalizes the quotation and service order process. By applying modern software development practices and spatial mapping techniques, the project seeks to enhance record accuracy, reduce retrieval time, and support data-driven decision-making for both product sales and service operations.











Project Context
Limen Auto Parts Center is an established family-owned auto parts retail shop located at 1308, 264 Epifanio de los Santos Avenue, Pasay City, Metro Manila. Since its inception thirteen years ago, the business has focused on providing genuine Mitsubishi car parts that are widely available and sought after in the Philippines. The shop is managed by Mr. Wilson Limen and Mrs. Angelica Limen, supported by their children and a dedicated staff of four individuals, for a total workforce of six members who handle sales, customer service, and stock management.
The physical layout of the shop consists of a two-floor commercial space. The first floor is utilized as the main sales area and display for high-demand items, while the second floor serves as the primary stockroom for additional inventory. Despite having a computer for digital pricelist checking, the majority of operational records—including inventory logs, sales receipts, and business reports—are maintained through traditional paper-based methods.
This reliance on manual operations has created several critical issues in the shop's daily activities. One common scenario occurs when a customer inquires about a specific auto part; staff members are often required to physically navigate between the two floors to verify stock availability, leading to significant customer waiting times. Furthermore, the lack of real-time visibility often results in "blind stockouts," where an item is listed as available in the manual log but is discovered to be missing only during the physical retrieval process.
The absence of a structured spatial mapping system also complicates the training of new staff and slows down item retrieval for experienced employees. Currently, the organization of parts across the two floors relies heavily on the memory of the long-term staff. A particularly repetitive struggle involves the issuance of sales receipts; the business must constantly purchase physical receipt booklets, and staff members are burdened with the manual transcription of every item sold. When sales volumes are high, the time spent manually calculating daily totals and transcribing these handwritten receipts into weekly reports becomes a major bottleneck, often leading to human errors and delayed identification of inventory movement patterns. These scenarios highlight the urgent need for a digitized system that integrates physical location data with real-time sales and automated receipt printing.



Objectives of the Study
The general objective of this study is to design, develop, test, and evaluate LimenAuto: A 3D-Spatial and AI-Enhanced Inventory Management System with Integrated POS for Limen Auto Parts Center.
The specific objectives are as follows:
1. Design the system by specifying its core capabilities and functional modules, which include:
- Barcode Scanning and Product Identification for rapid transaction entry.
- Point of Sale (POS) Transaction Processing with automatic thermal receipt generation to eliminate manual writing.
- Real-Time Inventory Tracking with automatic stock level adjustments.
- AI-Powered Store Manager and Natural Language Search: Utilizing the Gemini 1.5 Pro model to interpret conversational queries for inventory retrieval, sales analytics, and operational decision support.
- 3D Visualization of Shop Layout to assist in physical item location.
- Automated Report Generation for daily, weekly, and monthly business analysis.
- Role-Based User Access Control for Admin, Cashier, and Stock Personnel.
2. Develop the system using appropriate hardware and software technologies:
- Software Requirements: React.js for the frontend, Firebase (Firestore, Auth, Hosting) for the backend and database management, and Three.js for 3D visualization.
- Hardware Requirements: A computer system (minimum i5 processor, 8GB RAM), a barcode scanner (USB/Bluetooth), and a thermal receipt printer.
3. Test the system using comprehensive methodologies and tools to ensure functionality and reliability:
- Unit Testing: Using the Jest framework to verify individual components.
- Integration Testing: Using Postman for API validation and Cypress for end-to-end testing.
- User Acceptance Testing (UAT): Conducting hands-on sessions with Limen Auto Parts Center staff to validate real-world usability.
4. Evaluate the system based on the ISO 25010 Software Quality Model to determine its technical quality and user acceptability, focusing on characteristics such as:
- Functional Suitability
- Performance Efficiency
- Usability
- Reliability
- Security
- Maintainability
5. Prepare an implementation plan that outlines the transition from manual to digital operations, including deployment strategies and a user training program.

Purpose and Description
The purpose of this study is to provide Limen Auto Parts Center with a practical and innovative tool to transition from paper-based management to a modernized digital ecosystem. Research is needed because the current manual workflow limits the business's ability to respond quickly to customer needs and maintain perfect stock accuracy. By implementing this system, the shop will derive practical benefits such as reduced operational errors, faster transaction times, and better spatial organization of their two-floor inventory.
Description of the Proposed System
The system is a web-integrated application designed to unify sales and inventory management. Key capabilities include:
- Integrated POS and Barcode Support: Allows for seamless checkout and instant stock updates.
- 3D Spatial Visualization: Provides an interactive map of the stockroom, allowing users to "see" where items are located before physically going to get them.
- AI Store Manager: Leverages the Gemini 1.5 Pro model to act as an intelligent virtual assistant. It interprets natural language queries, allowing staff to "ask" the system for stock levels, sales snapshots, and item locations without navigating complex menus.
- Automated Reporting: Eliminates manual math by automatically generating sales and movement charts.
Beneficiaries
The study will be beneficial to the following:
- Limen Auto Parts Center Owners: Provides real-time data for better business planning and reduced stock loss.
- Shop Employees: Reduces physical fatigue and mental workload by streamlining item locating, eliminating the need to manually write receipts, and automating sales recording.
- Customers: Ensures faster service and more accurate confirmation of part availability.
- Student Proponents: Offers applied experience in high-level web technologies and AI implementation.

Scope and Limitation of the Study

Scope
1. User Management: Role-based access for Administrators, Stock Clerks, and Cashiers.
2. Inventory Module: Real-time tracking of stock levels, low-stock alerts, and batch entry via barcode scanning.
3. Sales & Payment: Integrated POS interface supporting Cash, Bank Transfers, and E-wallets (GCash/Maya) with automated receipt generation.
4. Spatial Visualization: A web-based 3D digital twin of the 2-floor stockroom for visual item location.
5. AI Integration: Utilization of Google Gemini 1.5 Pro to process natural language queries regarding stock status and sales reports.
Limitation
1. AI Reliability: The AI Store Manager provides probabilistic responses based on available data; extremely complex or ambiguous queries may require human verification.
2. Hardware & Platform: The application is a Progressive Web App (PWA) optimized for desktop and tablet Chrome browsers and is not a native iOS/Android application.
3. Scope of Automation: The system automates digital record-keeping but still requires manual physical placement of items to match the 3D coordinates encoded in the system.
4. System Boundary: It is designed for a single-branch operation; multi-branch synchronization and external e-commerce functionalities are not included.


Conceptual Framework
The study utilizes the Input-Process-Output (IPO) model to illustrate the system framework.
Input
- Knowledge Requirements: Inventory management principles, 3D spatial mapping logic, AI/NLP command processing, and web development frameworks.
- Software Requirements: Windows/macOS, VS Code, React.js, Firebase (Firestore/Auth/Hosting), and Three.js libraries.
- Hardware Requirements: Computer system (i5 processor, 8GB RAM), barcode scanner, and thermal receipt printer.
Process
Following the system development methodology, the process includes the following phases:
- Requirements Analysis: Gathering user needs and environmental data.
- System Design: UI/UX prototyping and database schema architectural planning.
- Development: Coding of the POS, 3D, and AI modules.
- Testing: Validation via unit, integration, and functional tests.
- Evaluation: Assessment using the ISO 25010 quality standards.
Output
The final output is the AI-Powered Spatial Inventory Management and Barcode-Integrated Point of Sale System with 3D Stockroom Visualization for Limen Auto Parts Center.

Definition of Terms
3D-Spatial Visualization: A digital, three-dimensional representation of the physical shop layout used to identify and manage the location of items.
AI Store Manager: An intelligent virtual assistant powered by the Gemini 1.5 Pro model that enables conversational interaction with the system's database for inventory lookup, sales performance analysis, and operational decision support.
Barcode Integration: The use of hardware scanners to read product codes for lightning-fast inventory and POS data entry.
Functional Suitability: An ISO 25010 metric that evaluates how well the system's functions meet the stated and implied needs of the user.
Point of Sale (POS): The specific location or interface where a retail transaction is completed and recorded.
Spatial Management: The organization and optimization of products within the physical dimensions of the store, assisted by digital mapping.
Direct Stock Update: The automatic adjustment of inventory quantities as soon as a sale is finalized in the POS module.
