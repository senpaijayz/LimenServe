**INTRODUCTION**



In the contemporary retail and service landscape, the strategic integration of digital technologies is no longer a luxury but an absolute operational necessity. This capstone project focuses on the comprehensive design and development of LimenServe, a sophisticated, web-based management information system explicitly engineered to modernize the intricate retail and service workflows of Limen Auto Parts Center. As an established enterprise catering to a diverse clientele with rigorous automotive demands, Limen Auto Parts Center requires a robust digital backbone capable of handling high-volume transactions, complex inventory matrices, and dynamic service requirements. 

LimenServe is architected using a state-of-the-art technology stack that heavily relies on modern JavaScript libraries and frameworks to build a highly interactive, responsive frontend interface. This user-centric presentation layer is seamlessly coupled with robust, highly secure backend Application Programming Interfaces (APIs) that flawlessly facilitate complex modular business logic. Furthermore, enterprise-grade cloud-based relational database hosting provides an immutable, centralized architecture for secure data storage, guaranteeing high availability and robust data integrity across multiple concurrent user sessions. 

Going beyond traditional numerical inventory systems, LimenServe introduces a pioneering approach to spatial awareness within the retail environment. The system securely integrates advanced WebGL libraries to render a highly functional, completely interactive three-dimensional (3D) visualization of the physical two-floor store layout forming a "digital twin" of the establishment. By seamlessly combining these cutting-edge core web technologies alongside proven traditional physical hardware peripherals—such as ruggedized barcode scanners for lightning-fast data entry and high-speed thermal printers for immediate raw receipt generation—the system creates a holistic digital ecosystem. Ultimately, LimenServe empowers the business with fully automated cost estimation, meticulously structured quotation generation, and instantaneous real-time point-of-sale processing to drastically streamline operations and elevate the overall customer experience to a premium level.

i. Project context.

The automotive parts retail and repair service industry is inherently complex, demanding precise orchestration of thousands of unique individual components, fluctuating labor variables, and rapid customer turnaround expectations. The primary existential problem currently faced by Limen Auto Parts Center is its heavy, almost total reliance on a fundamentally manual, overwhelmingly paper-based workflow. The business actively manages daily sales, intricate mechanical service requests, and an extensive, highly diverse inventory spanning thousands of distinct Stock Keeping Units (SKUs) completely distributed across a complex, massive two-floor commercial space. This incredibly traditional, archaic approach significantly restricts the business's overall structural capacity to consistently maintain perfect stock accuracy and safely scale operations. It severely handicaps their ability to decisively and swiftly respond to immediate customer pricing inquiries, primarily because all critical operational records—including raw daily inventory logs, supplier transaction histories, and hand-calculated mechanical quotation estimates—are deeply bound to fragile physical ledgers rather than a centralized, instantly searchable digital database.

Stemming directly from this profoundly burdensome manual workflow are several critical operational bottlenecks that severely degrade daily productivity, frustrate both employees and customers, and firmly justify the absolute, pressing need for this technological intervention. These challenges are characterized by an incredibly disruptive and frustratingly frequent occurrence of "blind stockouts," where physical parts are registered as available on paper logs but discovered to be missing only after manual inspection of the disparate storage sectors between the two floors, resulting in massive time wastage and customer dissatisfaction. 

Furthermore, the business faces intense operational friction due to the manual mathematical computation required for generating complex service and repair estimates. Cashiers must painstakingly cross-reference multiple paper pricelists and manually factor in variable labor costs, a convoluted process that leads to agonizingly slow customer response times, inconsistent pricing quotes between staff members, and an increased risk of severe mathematical errors affecting profitability. 

Finally, the highly repetitive and laborious manual transcription of handwritten sales receipts for every transaction introduces frequent human penmanship and calculation errors while drastically delaying the critical managerial identification of shifting inventory movement patterns. This makes data-driven restocking decisions structurally impossible, as business owners are left without real-time sales velocity insights.

**International Literature and Studies**
The transition from traditional, manual operations to modern digital Management Information Systems (MIS) has become an absolute necessity for retail enterprises globally aiming to maximize their operational efficiency. This centralized data paradigm is strictly necessary to drastically streamline complex supply chains, allowing executives to instantaneously optimize high-volume inventory levels based on shifting market demands.

In highly specialized servicing sectors, such as global automotive repair and parts distribution, the accuracy of cost estimation directly impacts customer retention and revenue. Research highlighted by Torque360 (2024) within the international automotive repair sector indicates that relying on manual mathematical computations for repair quotes structurally introduces a high margin of costly human error. Their studies prove that incorporating algorithm-driven, automated cost estimation modules into core enterprise software definitively mitigates these severe manual errors. This automation standardizes highly variable labor time estimations, accounts for instantaneous parts price fluctuations, and actively prevents lost revenue stemming from under-quoted mechanical jobs.

From an infrastructural standpoint, the debate between localized versus cloud connectivity strongly favors the latter. International comparative studies published by the ETP Group (2023) firmly establish that modern cloud-based Point of Sale (POS) solutions vastly outperform traditional, localized hardware systems. While legacy desktop databases offer a false sense of offline security, comprehensive modern cloud networks inherently deliver unparalleled remote accessibility, automated continuous data backups, and real-time multi-store channel synchronization that severely fragmented legacy offline setups simply cannot structurally support without colossal physical server costs.

Delving specifically into the intricacies of the automotive supply chain, aggregations formulated by Gitnux (2024) prove the immense value of specialized digital architecture. Cloud-reliant Enterprise Resource Planning (ERP) systems that are precisely tailored to flawlessly catalog millions of highly complex, cross-referenced automotive parts and distinct Stock Keeping Units (SKUs) drastically reduce the occurrence of "blind stockouts." By rigidly enforcing instantaneous database synchronization via Application Programming Interface (API) pathways, these massive digital systems prevent the catastrophic business failure of selling inventory that no longer physically exists on the shelf.

Finally, navigating massive physical inventory floors presents an immense logistical hurdle that numerical data alone cannot fully solve. Recent mechanical engineering and logistics validations by Mecalux and 3D Logistix (2023) conclusively demonstrate the transformative power of emerging Three-Dimensional (3D) visualizations and "digital twins" in warehouse operations. ii. System technical background.
In writing this technical background, the goal is to inform and educate the readers on the specific technicalities and jargon utilized in this project so that they will fully understand the literature and scope presented. A profoundly fundamental component of this modern retail architecture is the flawless integration of **Point of Sale (POS) terminals**. A POS is the specific interface where a retail transaction is completed. These are natively paired with **barcode scanners**, which are hardware devices that use optical sensors to read printed barcodes, eliminating the need for manual typing. 

Furthermore, to address vulnerabilities caused by internet interruptions in varying local Philippine network conditions, this system leverages **Progressive Web App (PWA)** technologies. A PWA is a type of application built using common web technologies (HTML, CSS, JavaScript) that behaves similarly to a natively installed desktop software, allowing for "offline-first" capabilities using local computer memory caching. 

Another critical term is **Application Programming Interface (API)**. An API is a set of rules and protocols that allow completely different software applications to communicate and seamlessly extract data from one another. By maintaining a clean structural decoupling through APIs, the frontend presentation of the application prevents massive performance bottlenecks and reliably pushes exact data changes instantly to all connected computers. Finally, the system leverages **WebGL**, a JavaScript API designed directly for intricately rendering high-performance interactive 3D and 2D graphics strictly within any compatible web browser without using cumbersome external plug-ins.
